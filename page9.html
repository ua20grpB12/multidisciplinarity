ART INSTALLATION

Which projects appeal to us the most conceptually and in the elaboration?

Archive of Mind, Salem US

Archive of Mind by Kimjoosa

Het werk van Kimsooja transformeert eenvoudige, alledaagse handelingen in momenten van meditatie en transcendentie. Archive of Mind is een participatieve installatie die in samenwerking met bezoekers in de loop van de tentoonstelling wordt opgebouwd. Museumbezoekers worden aangemoedigd om aan het grote werkoppervlak te zitten, hun hoofd leeg te maken van afleiding en weg te zinken in de essentiële ervaring om met hun eigen handen een bol klei te vormen. In de loop van de tentoonstelling worden duizenden kleibollen gegenereerd door kleine, individuele gebaren die de emotionele sporen van hun makers onthullen en cumulatief een complexe reeks van textuur, schaal en toon genereren.

Rainbow Circle, Singapore

Rainbow Circle by Suzzan Victor

Suzann Victor often addresses taboos and sensitive topics such as post-colonialism and gender issues, as well as the environment in response to space, context, architecture, and more. Suzann Victor's installations are often theatrical and on a large scale, allowing the visitor to enter the work."Rainbow Circle" is an objectless installation that allows a natural phenomenon to appear in a closed space. The installation was specially designed for the 2013 Singapore Biennale that had the theme "If the World Changed". The installation evokes awareness for sustainability and the environment, stimulating the viewer to imagine a future in which beauties from nature, such as a rainbow, become so rare that they can only be seen in a museum. It is an installation where art and science come together. Natural elements such as sunlight and water droplets are used in conjunction with a device that tracks the movement of the sun.

Les Deux Plateaux, Paris

Colonnes des Buren by Daniel Buren

Daniel Buren is mainly known as a conceptual artist, but besides that also as an abstract minimalist. Characteristic for Daniel Buren's work is the fixed use of vertical stripes, alternating between white and another color with a standard width of 8.7 cm. The artist has called these stripes his "outil visuel" since 1965.
In 1986 Daniel Buren created the controversial sculpture ‘Les Deux Plateaux’, better known as ‘Colonnes de Buren’, in the courtyard of the Palais-Royal in Paris. The 3,000 m2 art installation sparked a fierce debate about the integration of contemporary art and historic buildings. It was attacked for its expense and unsuitability for a historical monument. The architectural writer Andrew Ayers described the installation as; “Buren's work takes the form of a conceptual grid imposed on the courtyard, whose intersections are marked by candy-striped black-and-white columns of different heights poking up from the courtyard's floor like sticks of seaside rock. In one sense the installation can be read as an exploration of the perception and intellectual projection of space.” The columns contrast with the classicism of the Palais-Royal and the different heights create a visual rhythm. It is an invitation to the public to participate in the art installation by moving, sitting and climbing between the columns.
