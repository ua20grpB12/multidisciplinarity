PROCESS

REFERENCES

To start of with the process we discussed and analyzed references and practical examples of multidisciplinary working and thinking, from the past and present. By looking for those references we looked for cases, projects, works, ideas, person and trends that inspired us and we asked ourselves the question why those particular references impressed us. Based on the found references in the context of multidisciplinarity on the one hand and installation art on the other, we discussed which images had the greatest potential according to us and would contribute most in our multidisciplinary creative process. We subdivided them into 3 categories: Multidisciplinarity, Art installations and Covid-19.

CONCLUSION

In response to the found references and the synthesis that followed, we started the conversation again. We all found certain elements from the references interesting as well relevant to the theme of this case study, which is also the theme of our daily lives today: the corona virus. We concluded that we found certain projects and installations interesting because of their dynamic nature and the possibility of participation. Interaction with the audience often arouses curiosity, it keeps them on their toes and perhaps stimulates them to act or think. Examples are Archive of Mind, Colonnes de Buren and Elevated Gallery / Powerless structures. We as a group could consider the possibilities of this in our project. Participation seems difficult in times of social distancing, so there is plenty of food for thought and brainstorming here. Are there possibilities to make this possible in a responsible way? And if so, what are these?

Based on the references, perception is also important to us. Depending on the context and the situation in which we live, our perception and experience of certain things changes. We interpret things differently according to a change of context. Ceal Floyer inspires us in this topic. A shopping list could now evoke completely different experiences and interpretations than in the pre-corona times. You could think of the many old people who are very lonely because of corona, and for whom the supermarket is the only moment of social contact in a day.
