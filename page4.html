PROCESS

HYPOTHESIS

What is the societal social impact of COVID-19? And how are a private / semi-public / public space, a digital / physical space, an individual / collective and a perception of time part of this?

To answer these questions we use the model of Pierce, along various multidisciplines and views. A free form of interpretation stimulates this impact.

The casestudy Multidisciplinarity is a casestudie where multiple disciplines come together, where multiple disciplines have a conversation. In this case study we process different disciplines, including: sculpture art, installation art, performance art, furniture function, film art, video art and scenography designed through visual arts.

In this case study we research the social effect of COVID-19 on an individual as well as the collective. We dissect the understanding of how an individual relates to the collective and how the collective relates to an individual. To research this, we work along a self-constructed supporting structure. A structure in which multiple social focuses are reduced to, one in our view, the most linear focus of COVID 19; the social impact. We will explain how one person makes a difference in the greater whole or how a single person resists the greater whole.

We will also look within this set-up structure how time and space participate in this. We are going to look at space (the physical space) with reference to a private space, semi-public and public space. And on the other hand, the non-concrete perceptible space (the digital space) and how these physical and digital spaces are interwoven in an era like this.

We can say that at this time, for the first time in history, man can be in multiple spaces at the same time during one time frame (again digital vs. physical space). This includes the individual or private space (kot, house), the semi-public space (supermarket, school, museum) and the public space (quays, streets, squares)

We are going to look at how these three quires of space have gotten a new meaning during this time. We can say that an individual space can, for example, become a semi-public space by adding the digital dimension (think of online chat rooms, BB Collaborate, etc.). Conversely, it can also be stated that a public space can get the experience of an individual space, think of walking alone through the empty streets. This gives the public space an individual character, whereby an individual is motivated to master this public space and make it his own. This discussed spatial aspect is linear in time. Time is relative in this aspect, because this public space has several owners over a 24-hour period.

The emotional idea of being alone but still together is stimulated by the aforementioned factors: the social aspect, the spatial aspect, the time aspect and the interpretation of these aspects. To develop an understandable framework for these aspects we use the model of Pierce. Due to COVID-19, the aspects are given a new meaning in this time frame. (For example, the public space is still the same as it has always been, only now one looks at it individually or as a collective differently). This layering in the model of Pierce is incorporated per aspect in our installation.

As mentioned earlier, we will process multiple disciplines, in multiple spaces, in multiple time frames, in one social aspect in our final product as an installation. The reinterpretation from the individual forms the centre, in which it is urged to look differently at consciousness, oriented from the subconscious. The communication of this installation, interwoven with the model of Pierce, the time view, the spatial view and the social view, are summarized in a self-developed beak figure. This figure is the theoretical supporting structure, serving for the ultimate autonomous concept. The tension between theory, concept and the autonomous is incorporated into the final product.
