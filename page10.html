MULTIDISCIPLINARITY

Which projects contain the combination of different disciplines, multidisciplinarity, and can we best apply to this casestudy?

Elevated Gallery, Denmark

Elevated Gallery / Powerless Structures by Michael Elmgreen & Ingar Dragset

Elmgreen & Dragset are known for their wide range of art pieces which distinguish themselves from each other through great diversity. Their art pieces generally have a very playful and humoristic approach, yet they have a critical undertone. This combination of playfulness and seriousness is what makes their work so interesting. Since 1997 they have been producing a wide range of pieces in an ongoing series called "Powerless Structures". These pieces are characterized by radical displacements of spatial constructions/objects that change the entire context of those constructions. These everyday constructions/objects are transformed into installations that encourage reflection. "Elevated Gallery" is one of these works, where a gallery is suspended from the ceiling. The suspension of the architectural structure encourages a completely different experience, this due to the fact that the dynamic between the construction and the environment is changed. The multidisciplinary way of working of Elmgreen & Dragset is clearly visible here. The interwovenness between architecture and installation-art is clearly visible. Also, the participation of the visitor plays a role in this piece. 

The Villagers, Antwerp

The Villagers by Rinus van de Velde

In his project 'The Villagers’, Rinus van de Velde shows that he is at home in various disciplines. He creates interiors from painted cardboard that gives the illusion that they are real yet they are not. When walking trough this art installation, you get a real life experience from the space and objects, due to it’s human scale. Around you a story unfolds. In the adjacent room you can watch a film taking place in this cardboard world. An interesting aspect is that Rinus not only created this world but also wrote the underlying story and acted it out. The result is a combination of interior architecture, installation art and film. This art work served as an inspiration for our project; especially the way he plays with peoples’ perception and the manner in which he intertwines multiple disciplines exulting in a higher level of art. We could pose the question: ‘What if we add even more dimensions by filming the experiences of people to this art project and show them their reaction to it.

Pelléas et Mélisande, Antwerp

Pelléas et Mélisande by Claude Debussy

Originally, Pelléas et Mélisande was a play by the Belgian Maurice Maeterlinck. In 1902, Claude Debussy adapted it into an opera. In 2018, this piece was reperformed in Opera Vlaanderen in the city of Antwerp. Exactly one century after Claude Debussy's year of death. The opera tells a tragic story about a love triangle between two brothers named Golaud and Pelléas, and a woman named Mélisande. The Opera Ballet Vlaanderen itself calls the piece “an evocative and highly poetic musical reflection on man’s the state of being”.

Choreographers Sidi Larbi Cherkaoui and Damian Jalet worked together with performance artist Marina Abramovic. Abramovic took care of the production’s scenography. The costumes were designed by fashion designer Iris van Herpen. All of these artists worked very closely together for the realization of this production. The musical direction was done by Alejo Pérez. Dance, music and performance art (which already has a multidisciplinary character in itself because it is a combination between theater, music and fine arts) are being combined here. To this we can also add the scenography (set design) and costume design. Therefore, this production is an amalgamation of many disciplines.
